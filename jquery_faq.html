Who is the director of the National Park Service?
Shawn Benge, Deputy Director, Operations, is exercising the delegated authority of the Director of the National Park Service.
How many employees are in the National Park Service?
Permanent, temporary, and seasonal employees: Approximately 20,000
How many areas are in the National Park System?
The system includes 423 areas covering more than 85 million acres in every state.
How many people visit the national parks?
Total recreation visitors to the national parks in 2019: 327,516,619.
Can I bring my service dog to a national park?
Yes, service dogs are legally permitted anywhere in a park that visitors can go.
How do I apply for a job with the National Park Service?
National Park Service jobs, including both permanent and seasonal positions, are listed on USAJOBs.
What government agency oversees the National Park Service?
The National Park Service is a bureau of the Department of the Interior. Directly overseeing its operation is the department's Assistant Secretary for Fish, Wildlife and Parks.
How old is the National Park System?
The National Park Service was created by an act signed by President Woodrow Wilson on August 25, 1916. Yellowstone National Park was established by an act signed by President Ulysses S. Grant on March 1, 1872, as the nation's first national park.
What is the most-visited national park?
Blue Ridge Parkway with 14.1 million visits in 2020
What is the National Park Service budget?
FY 2017 Enacted: $2.932 billion

Park facts

Yellowstone National Park

  • Oldest US national Park
  • Is home to more than 500 active geysers (more than half the world’s geysers).
  • Home to the largest concentration of mammals in the lower 48 states (67 species).
  • There are approximately 1,000 miles of hiking trails in Yellowstone.

Yosemite

  • Has 3 of 10 highest waterfalls in the world
  • Ribbon Falls in Yosemite is 9x taller than the Niagara Falls
  • Yosemite is the only national park to bid to host the winter Olympics.
  • Yosemite is one of the few places in the U.S. where you can see a rainbow at night.

Grand Canyon

  • One of the Seven Wonders of the World
  • There are an estimated 1,000 caves within the canyon, but only 335 have been recorded.
  • There’s a town inside the canyon with a population of 208.
  • The canyon is home to pink rattlesnakes
